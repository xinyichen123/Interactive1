The environmental impact of 1.5-2°C riset
Warming of 1.5 to 2°C will lead to a reduction of rainforest biomass and will increase deforestation and wildfires. Trees at the southern boundaries of boreal forests will die. The IPCC Special Report states, with medium confidence, that at an increased level of warming between 1.5 and 2°C, instabilities in the Antarctic ice sheet and/or the irreversible loss of the Greenland ice sheet could lead to multi-meter (greater than 6 feet) sea level rise over a time scale of hundreds to thousands of years.
